<p>
      No single tool is right for every agency. Commercial platforms offer convenience
      and vendor support but come with ongoing costs, data residency concerns, and limited
      customisation. General-purpose open source tools offer full control but require
      significant technical capacity. KoNote is designed for a specific niche: Canadian
      social service nonprofits that need outcome tracking built on research, privacy
      compliance built into the architecture, and costs that a five-person agency can
      sustain — without requiring an enterprise IT team.
</p>

<h2>When KoNote Is the Right Fit</h2>

<div class="two-col">
<div>
<h3>Good fit</h3>
<ul class="check-list">
<li>Social service agencies tracking participant outcomes</li>
<li>Nonprofits that need to demonstrate program impact to funders</li>
<li>Organisations requiring Canadian data residency (PIPEDA/PHIPA)</li>
<li>Programs that value participant feedback as part of service delivery</li>
<li>Agencies that want full control over their data</li>
<li>Programs serving up to ~2,000 active participants</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
<h3>Not the right fit</h3>
<ul class="x-list">
<li>Agencies requiring billing, invoicing, or clinical assessments</li>
<li>Teams with zero technical capacity and no IT partner</li>
<li>Organisations wanting a fully managed SaaS solution with phone support</li>
<li>Programs needing native mobile apps or video conferencing</li>
<li>Large enterprises requiring tens of thousands of concurrent users</li>
</ul>
